# Economy Rebalance Phases

This rollout keeps the sticker economy generous enough to fund large pack volume while moving repeatable Ryo generation toward active play instead of passive training abuse.

## Phase 1: Battle Rewards

- Win payout: `1000` Ryo
- Loss payout: `350` Ryo
- Goal: make battles the default repeatable money loop for pack spending

## Phase 2: Training Ryo Reduction

- Training-caused level-ups now pay `25%` of the normal level-up Ryo
- Battle-caused level-ups still pay full value
- Goal: keep training useful for progression without letting it outscale active play

## Phase 3: Training Slot Progression

- Base training capacity: `10` slots
- Trainer Level `10` unlocks purchases up to `20` total slots
- Trainer Level `25` unlocks purchases up to `30` total slots
- Trainer Level `40` unlocks purchases up to `40` total slots

Upgrade ladder:

- `15` slots for `10,000` Ryo
- `20` slots for `20,000` Ryo
- `30` slots for `40,000` Ryo
- `40` slots for `80,000` Ryo

Goal:

- preserve training as a real roster-building feature
- add a long-term Ryo sink
- keep very large training farms in the late game, not the early game

## Phase 4: Bond Bonus Sell Values

Sell tiers:

- Levels `1-50`: `1500` Ryo base value
- Levels `51-90`: `2500` Ryo base value
- Levels `91-99`: `3000` Ryo base value
- Level `100`: `5000` Ryo base value

Bond Bonus protection:

- Levels `1-9` cash out at `25%` of the base value
- Level `10` unlocks the full Bond Bonus value

UI framing:

- never call this a penalty
- always present the missing value as a locked Bond Bonus

Goal:

- stop expedition-to-sell loops from becoming free profit
- keep high-level releases valuable
- preserve a friendly player-facing message

## Phase 5: Expedition Quests

Daily quest templates:

- Complete 1 expedition: `300` Ryo
- Complete 2 expeditions: `700` Ryo
- Bond 1 Pokemon: `250` Ryo + `20` Dust
- Bond 3 Pokemon: `400` Ryo + `1` Trail Mix

Weekly quest templates:

- Complete 5 expeditions: `2500` Ryo
- Complete 10 expeditions: `5000` Ryo
- Bond Pokemon from 3 different types: `2000` Ryo + `50` Dust

Goal:

- reward expedition activity directly
- reinforce the collection loop without making expeditions themselves free

## Phase 6: Future Expedition Scaling

Gen 1 zones stay at current costs to keep early collection accessible.

Suggested generation cost multipliers:

- Gen 2: `1.5x`
- Gen 3: `2.0x`
- Gen 4: `2.75x`
- Gen 5: `3.5x`
- Gen 6+: scale by rarity band and zone identity, not only generation number

Recommended zone roles:

- common routes: low cost, broad encounter tables
- specialist biomes: mid cost, stronger type targeting
- high-rarity sanctuaries: high cost, low encounter count, premium chase targets

Goal:

- keep Gen 1 approachable
- make later generations aspirational
- let new content naturally absorb more late-game Ryo

## Phase 7: Dungeon and Gym Runs

This becomes the high-engagement endgame faucet.

Core structure:

- one run contains `10-20` consecutive teams
- each dungeon or gym line pushes different roster needs
- deeper clears raise reward quality and payout
- partial clears still pay out

Reward shape:

- early floors: mostly Ryo
- mid floors: Ryo + Dust
- late floors: Ryo + Dust + Candy
- boss clears: bonus chest, pack token, or rare material

Design goals:

- give players a healthy "I need Ryo, let me grind" mode
- reward broad team building instead of one optimized roster
- make type diversity matter for long-run success
